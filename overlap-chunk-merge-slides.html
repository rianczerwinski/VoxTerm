Overlapping-Chunk LocalAgreement

A consensus-based transcription pipeline for VoxTerm

Adapted from vbuterin/stt-daemon — based on the whisper-streaming algorithm by Maciej Pióro (UFAL)

PR #34 · VoxTerm · press → or click to advance

The Problem

Fire-and-Forget (before)

  • Buffer audio until silence
  • Transcribe the entire chunk once
  • Clear buffer, display result
  • High latency — nothing shows until silence
  • Hallucinated tail words persist in output

LocalAgreement (after)

  • Transcribe every 1.5s in overlapping windows
  • Only commit words two ticks agree on
  • Trim buffer to last committed word
  • Words appear incrementally as confirmed
  • Hallucinated tails filtered automatically

Core Idea: Two Ticks Must Agree

A word is committed to final output only when the previous tick's hypothesis and the current tick's hypothesis agree on it at the front.

Each Tick: Step by Step

1

Read audio buffer

Non-destructive read — the buffer is a sliding window, not cleared. get_audio() returns accumulated audio since last trim.

2

Transcribe

MLX Qwen3-ASR transcribes the window. Produces a new word sequence (the hypothesis for this tick).

3

Strip re-transcribed prefix

Since trimming is approximate, the transcriber may re-produce committed words. _strip_retranscribed() detects and removes them.

4

Agreement & commit

Compare with previous hypothesis. The longest common prefix is committed. Remaining words become the new hypothesis.

5

Trim audio buffer

trim_front() slides the window forward past committed audio, keeping a 0.5s safety margin.

6

Display

Committed words are final. Pending hypothesis shown as provisional (can change next tick).

The 0.5s safety margin ensures the transcriber has context overlap between ticks.

Silence Handling & Flush

  • Speech detected → ticks fire every 1.5s, agreement runs
  • Extended silence (> 1.0s) with pending hypothesis → flush_all() force-commits remaining words
  • Extended silence with no pending → reset state, stop buffering
  • Recording stops → flush any remaining hypothesis before final output

Handling Approximate Trims

Audio trimming is sample-based, not word-boundary-aligned. The transcriber may re-produce words that were already committed. _strip_retranscribed() handles this.

Summary

  • Lower latency — words appear as they're confirmed, not after silence
  • Fewer hallucinations — speculative tail words filtered by consensus
  • Sliding window — buffer never cleared entirely, always has context
  • Graceful flush — silence or stop commits remaining hypothesis
  • Robust to imprecise trims — re-transcribed prefixes detected and stripped
